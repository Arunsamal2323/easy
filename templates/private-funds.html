What is Private Funding

A private funding, also known as a private loan or private mortgage, is a loan agreement between two individuals or entities, where one party (the lender) provides funds to another party (the borrower) in exchange for a mortgage on a property. Private mortgages are an alternative to traditional bank mortgages and are typically facilitated by private investors, individuals, or private lending institutions.

Why Private

Private funding often come into play when borrowers have difficulty qualifying for traditional bank financing due to factors such as poor credit history, unconventional income sources, or unique property situations.

Benefits of Private Money

Private money can offer more flexibility with loan terms, repayment schedules, and eligibility criteria compared to traditional mortgages. In a private mortgage, the terms and conditions, including the interest rate, repayment period, and collateral, are negotiated between the lender and borrower.
